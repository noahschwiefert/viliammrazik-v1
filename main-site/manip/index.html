INFIL MANIPULATION
>TPU filament

In this project, my focus was on a specific aspect of 3D printing: infill structures. These structures serve as the internal fillings of 3D printed objects, enhancing their strength, conserving materials, and reducing overall weight. My project involved manipulating these infill structures by removing the standard outer layer of an average 3D printed object, thereby exposing the inside.
The final samples also demonstrate various infill densities incorporated into unified structures to control their movement, flexibility and shore hardness. As a demonstration of this method, I 3D printed the outsole of a shoe using a 3D scan of my foot. Utilizing my footprint, I customized the infill densities within the structure of the outsole, rendering it a fully personalized object. After creating the outsole, I hand-stitched an upper to complete the entire silhouette.
